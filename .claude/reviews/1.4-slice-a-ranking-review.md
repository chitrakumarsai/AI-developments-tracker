## Summary
Slice A introduces a pure, well-tested ranking module (`0.5·recency + 0.5·popularity`,
per-source-normalized) and wires it into the feed as the default "Relevant" order with a
recency-window control. Code is clean, immutable, documented, and secure. No CRITICAL/HIGH
issues. One MEDIUM (dead code) and two LOW notes below.

## Findings

### CRITICAL
None.

### HIGH
None.

### MEDIUM
- **`windowDaysFor` is dead code** — `lib/feed/queries.ts:61`. Exported but referenced
  nowhere (code or tests). It also duplicates the inline `WINDOW_DAYS[window] ?? null` used
  at line 80, and drifts from it: `windowDaysFor` defaults an unknown window to
  DEFAULT_WINDOW's 30 days, while the inline version defaults to `null`. Remove it, or make
  `getFeedItems` call it so there's one source of truth.

### LOW
- **Popularity pool truncation** — `lib/feed/queries.ts:92-101`. Relevant sort ranks the 300
  most-recent items within the window. A very popular but older item outside that window's
  300 newest is excluded from ranking. Documented as a deliberate speed tradeoff; fine for
  now, revisit if a window routinely exceeds 300 items (Slice B backfill makes this more
  likely).
- **Changing section resets sort to Relevant** — `app/page.tsx:104`. Category nav links hard-code
  `"relevant"`, so switching sections drops a `Newest`/`Top-starred` selection. This is
  reasonable (Top-starred is invalid outside repos/models) but is a silent UX choice worth a
  one-line comment.
